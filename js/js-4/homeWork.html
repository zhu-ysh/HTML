The word “horse-power” was first used two hundred years ago. James Watt from
a worker‟s family made the world first widely used steam engine. At first, he couldn‟t
tell people how powerful it was, because there were no units at that time. Watt decided
to find out how much work one strong horse could do in one minute. He named that
unit one horse-power. In this way he could measure the work of his steam engine.
